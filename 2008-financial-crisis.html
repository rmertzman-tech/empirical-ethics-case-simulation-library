P-SAGE: The Structural Finding

Sign intact

Banks lent. Mortgages were originated. CDOs were rated. Institutions were licensed and regulated. Surface operation continued through 2007.

Signal accessed

The credit-risk channel was accessed through deregulated securitization architecture and rating-agency assignment.

Interpretant replaced

Original: mortgage = creditworthiness assessment for sustainable homeownership. Replaced: mortgage = securitization feedstock with externalized risk.

φ consequence

The crash made substrate damage visible: household wealth loss, foreclosure cascades, neighborhood decline, and unequal burden-bearing.

Recovery: T1 vs T2

T2 response

TARP, quantitative easing, and compliance expansion can stabilize liquidity and restore operation without changing who controls the mission-critical channel.

T1 structural repair

Glass-Steagall equivalent, rating-agency restructuring, and originate-to-hold requirements alter the access condition itself.

φ recovery gap

Financial-system indicators can recover faster than households, neighborhoods, and communities that bore the substrate consequence.

Classroom contrast

Run the T2 era, then toggle T1. Ask students what changes in the model and what remains hidden in a T2-only repair.

The System Today

Sign intact

The financial system operates. Banks lend. Mortgage markets function.

Signal still vulnerable

The source app argues that the access condition shifted rather than disappeared.

BioBond / AMC response

Condition capital access on measurable FHRI maintenance, beneficial ownership disclosure, and φ early-warning indicators.

Maathai principle

CDFIs are offered as an alternative architecture: lower ε, community accountability, and genuine creditworthiness assessment.
