Teaching purpose

The unified update lets you install one package that includes both the Critical Thinking Bridge and the extracted Toulmin Argument Studio.

Recommended classroom rhythm

  1. Begin with the public case or simulation.
  2. Ask students what the case makes visible or morally urgent, without requiring personal disclosure.
  3. Translate that reaction into a Toulmin claim.
  4. Choose an ethical lens to identify the warrant and backing.
  5. Add a qualifier and rebuttal.
  6. End with an OS2 coordination statement: what can we examine, repair, or test together?

Lens table

LensWhat it noticesWorks whenFails when
Socrates
Dialogical Ethics / Examined Life		Confusion, unexamined claims, overconfidence, and the gap between appearing wise and being wise.Students need to slow down, define terms, expose hidden assumptions, or move from slogans to inquiry.Urgent protective action is required and endless questioning becomes avoidance.
Aristotle
Virtue Ethics / Practical Wisdom		Character formation, habits, flourishing, institutions that cultivate or deform practical judgment.The case concerns professional formation, institutional culture, role habits, courage, or judgment under pressure.Structural coercion is so strong that individual virtue language hides institutional responsibility.
Kant
Deontological Ethics / Respect for Persons		Dignity, consent, deception, universalizable rules, and treating persons as ends rather than mere means.The case involves deception, coercion, consent failure, or exploitation of vulnerable persons.The case requires fine-grained outcome comparison or tradeoff analysis beyond rule-focused judgment.
Bentham / Mill
Consequentialism / Harm and Welfare		Aggregate harm, benefit, risk distribution, preventable suffering, and long-term consequences.Students need to compare harms, benefits, risk transfer, and policy outcomes.Aggregate benefit language hides rights violations or sacrifices vulnerable minorities.
Martha Nussbaum
Capabilities Approach		The gap between formal rights and real agency-supporting conditions.The case concerns education, health, care, disability, poverty, vulnerability, or institutional exclusion.Capabilities are listed abstractly without attention to local agency, culture, and implementation.
John Rawls
Social Contract / Fairness		Basic fairness, institutional design, arbitrary advantage, and whether rules could be accepted from behind a veil of ignorance.The case requires evaluating institutional rules, burdens, access, fairness, and public justification.Formal fairness language overlooks relational care, historical exclusion, or lived trauma.
Care Ethics
Relational Ethics / Vulnerability		Dependency, unequal burden, voice, care labor, abandonment, and relational repair.The case centers care, aging, illness, education, families, professional dependency, or frontline labor.Care language becomes sentimental and does not address power, law, money, or institutional design.
Wangari Maathai
Functional Equivalence / Environmental and Civic Repair		Shared practical tasks that different communities can endorse for different authentic reasons.The case needs civic coordination, repair, ecological thinking, or cross-community cooperation.Shared-task language hides coercion, unequal burden, or suppressed dissent.
Miranda Fricker
Epistemic Justice		Whose knowledge is heard, dismissed, mistrusted, or made unintelligible by institutional patterns.The case concerns testimony, warning, professional voice, marginalized knowledge, or credibility gaps.Epistemic injustice is treated only as interpersonal disrespect rather than as institutional structure.
