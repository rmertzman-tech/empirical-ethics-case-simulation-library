Minimal teaching route

  1. Introduce formal rights versus actual capabilities.
  2. Use the civic coordination Repair preset.
  3. Ask how different PRFs can support the same shared task for different authentic reasons.
  4. Connect to John Hope Franklin as the emotional anchor.

Full teaching route

  1. Run civic coordination with Repair preset.
  2. Open GEO Florida as the empirical justice case.
  3. Use Higher Education IPS as a rights-to-capabilities extension.
  4. Ask students to distinguish Rawlsian fairness, Nussbaum capabilities, and functional equivalence.

Suggested public discussion starters

Note: Your instructor will tell you which questions, if any, you should answer for class. The prompts below are examples for reflection and discussion.

  • What is the difference between having a formal right and having the actual capability to use it?
  • How can different communities cooperate without sharing the same worldview?
  • What does the moral commons require if justice is more than formal permission?

Teaching FHRI reminder:
