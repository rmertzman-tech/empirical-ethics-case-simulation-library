Core public question: What would it take to move a captive-population institution from extraction architecture toward moral-commons repair?

Teaching purpose: Students learn why formal operation and accreditation do not necessarily mean that the institution still serves its original rehabilitative Interpretant. They then test whether repair requires changing the ownership, financing, measurement, and public-accountability structure.

Responsibility caution:

This case is designed to shift analysis away from blaming frontline officers, program counselors, or incarcerated people and toward the ownership and governance architecture that controls mission-critical budget channels.

P-SAGE: The Structural Finding

Sign intact

Facilities operate. Inmates are housed. Staff are employed. DOC contracts are fulfilled. Accreditation can remain high at the Sign level.

Signal accessed

The programming budget allocation channel is accessed through ownership structure, REIT-era dividend logic, debt inheritance, and contract architecture.

Interpretant replaced

Original: per-diem as public investment in rehabilitation and public safety. Replaced: per-diem as contracted revenue for debt service and shareholder return.

Responsible address

The target of repair is the ownership and governance architecture, not the frontline worker or incarcerated person living inside the structure.

BioBond Transition Design

The BioBond is presented as a T1 instrument because it changes the capital-access and ownership conditions, not merely the performance language inside the existing structure.

Chapter 5 connection:

Formal rights and formal contracts are not enough. The question is whether actual capability-supporting conditions are distributed to incarcerated people, staff, families, and communities.

The Maathai Bottom-Up Pathway

The case uses Maathai not as a decorative analogy but as a strategy: build and scale low-epsilon alternatives until the existing institution cannot ignore what has become measurable and viable.

Functional equivalence:

Different communities can support the same repair pathway for different authentic reasons: public safety, human dignity, fiscal prudence, family stability, faith commitments, workforce health, or democratic accountability.

Scale-Free Comparison

The source app argues that GEO Group Florida and PE-acquired nursing homes show nearly identical structural ceilings despite different sectors, populations, regulations, and stated missions.

Reviewer caution:

The scale-free claim is powerful but should remain a research hypothesis until all cross-sector data, formulas, and source documents are independently verified.
