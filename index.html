What this site does

EECSL gives students public cases for seeing how institutional structure, data, incentives, hidden burdens, and repair conditions shape ethical life. It is not a private journal and it is not a certified audit system.

Privacy boundary:

Do not enter private personal information on this public site. Private reflection belongs in the Navigator, a personal notebook, or an instructor-approved course space.

Teaching-proxy governance:

Simulation outputs are prompts for disciplined ethical analysis. They are not certified audit results or automatic moral proof. Reviewer-facing empirical claims require source verification.

Evidence governance

How students should read the evidence labels

  • Type 1 public-source claim: A claim that should be traceable to public records, reports, filings, or other direct sources.
  • Type 3 framework application: A claim produced by applying an EBB framework, IPS model, FHRI-style teaching proxy, or visible-ledger interpretation.
  • Teaching proxy: A classroom model for disciplined reasoning, not a certified measurement instrument.
