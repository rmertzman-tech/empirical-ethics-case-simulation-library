Chapter Teaching Routes

These routes show how empirical cases can be reused across the EBB course without making every chapter feel formulaic.

Chapter 1 — Ethical Perception, Complexity, and the Need for Models

Capability Lab: Use the Schelling emergence simulation as the first demonstration of why ethical outcomes can exceed individual intentions.

EECSL: Preview the EECSL lightly; later chapters use empirical simulations to examine real institutions.

Chapter 3 — Evidence Policy, Warning, and Ethical Lenses

Capability Lab: Use the Challenger / Boisjoly classroom run mode.

EECSL: Open the 2008 Financial Crisis case as a larger-scale example of evidence-channel failure.

Chapter 4 — Systems, Emergence, and Responsibility

Capability Lab: Use Schelling and coordination-under-heat to show layered emergence.

EECSL: Use Higher Education IPS or 2008 Financial Crisis to show modeled parameters as empirical structural questions.

Chapter 5 — Social Contract, Justice, and the Moral Commons

Capability Lab: Use the civic coordination route with Repair preset.

EECSL: Use GEO Florida and Higher Education IPS to connect Rawls, Nussbaum, functional equivalence, Franklin, and Maathai-style coordination.

Chapter 12 — Systems, Governance, Care, and Institutional Risk

Capability Lab: Use coordination-under-heat and evidence-policy framing.

EECSL: Use 2008 Financial Crisis and Florida Nursing Homes as paired cases.

Capstone — Comparative Structural Ethics

Capability Lab: Use the active analysis toolbelt sequence.

EECSL: Use all four empirical case modules in comparison.

Claim TypeMeaningStudent Question
Direct public dataA value reported by a public source or dataset.Where did the number come from?
Computed model outputA score or result calculated from stated formulas.How was it computed?
Interpretive model claimAn ethical or structural diagnosis drawn from the model.What assumptions connect the result to the interpretation?

Navigator Boundary

The EECSL can include Navigator bridge prompts, but it should not become the Navigator. Students should not enter private personal details into public case pages.

Standard bridge language:

Use this public case structure privately. Do not enter private personal details into this public case page. Use the Navigator, private notes, or your course notebook for personal application.
